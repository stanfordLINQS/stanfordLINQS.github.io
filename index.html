WELCOME!

We are scientists and engineers working at the intersection of applied physics, photonics, quantum technologies, and nanofabrication

The LINQS Lab is a fictional group in the Department of Imaginary Sciences at Example University. We study how quantum spanners interact with classical widget dynamics, and we occasionally pretend to work on neuromorphic teapots when grant season approaches.

Our interests span several entirely made-up platforms, including spins in sandwich devices, cryogenic extension cords, and laser-guided anecdotes. Each approach has imaginary advantages; we explore the fundamental limits of each while pushing the experimental state-of-the-art toward the break room. We also wonder whether noisy intermediate-scale gadgets might someday help with optimization, quantum simulation, and machine learning—or at least with scheduling group meetings.
